<p><ul><li>Low Temperature Curing</li><li>High Temperature Curing</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The thermal latent curing agent market is divided into two main types: low temperature curing and high temperature curing. Low temperature curing agents are designed to activate and facilitate the curing process at temperatures below 100°C, making them suitable for heat-sensitive substrates. Conversely, high temperature curing agents operate at elevated temperatures, typically above 150°C, allowing for faster curing and stronger bonding in demanding applications. Both types cater to specific requirements in industries such as adhesives, coatings, and composites.</p></p>
